The rise of AI-driven automation is challenging established IT vendors and reshaping legacy systems. IBM’s 13% stock drop reflects market concerns over AI’s ability to automate tasks like COBOL modernization, potentially disrupting vendor lock-in models. However, human-centric leadership remains critical. Microsoft’s new Gaming CEO, Asha Sharma, explicitly rejects "bad AI," emphasizing emotional resonance in gaming over technological overreach. This stance highlights a growing tension: while AI promises efficiency, human creativity and oversight are still prioritized in high-stakes domains.  

In parallel, the commoditization of consumer electronics forces manufacturers to pivot. This shift underscores the need for tech leaders to adapt to global supply chain dynamics, balancing cost efficiency with brand differentiation.
