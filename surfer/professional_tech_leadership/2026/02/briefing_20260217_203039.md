## Macro Spotlight – Deep Dive  
### AI’s Impact on Workforce and Supply Chains  

The integration of AI into software development and legacy systems is fundamentally reshaping roles and supply chains. Anthropic’s CEO, Dario Amodei, describes the current collaboration between AI and human engineers as a "centaur phase," but predicts this will soon give way to AI dominance in many tasks. This transition will likely reduce the demand for entry-level software engineering positions, forcing companies to adapt quickly or risk obsolescence. For example, companies like Ford are leveraging AI to optimize EV production, focusing on battery efficiency and aerodynamics to achieve affordability targets.  

Simultaneously, the music industry’s "less-is-more" era highlights the broader economic pressures of AI-driven content saturation. With streaming platforms hosting 253 million songs and 106,000 new uploads daily, 88% of tracks receive fewer than 1,000 plays annually. This oversaturation forces industry consolidation and stricter quality controls, as seen in Universal Music Group’s acquisition attempts. The result is a bifurcated market: a small segment of highly polished, algorithm-friendly music and the vast majority of creative work that remains unseen.  

These trends underscore the need for leaders to balance innovation with risk management. While AI enables efficiency and new capabilities, it also introduces ethical challenges, such as data privacy concerns (e.g., the European Parliament’s ban on AI tools) and workforce displacement. Leaders must prioritize upskilling, ethical AI deployment, and strategic investments in legacy systems to navigate these shifts.
