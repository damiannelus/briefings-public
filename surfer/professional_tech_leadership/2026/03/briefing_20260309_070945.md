## Macro Spotlight – Deep Dive  

### Strategic Thread: Geopolitical and Legal Forces Driving Tech Innovation and Risk  

The ongoing **US tariffs on imported EVs** have forced Hyundai and Kia to discontinue their cheapest models, reflecting a broader trend where trade policies directly influence product strategies. This decision stems from a combination of tariff pressures and weak sales, signaling that manufacturers are prioritizing profitability over market expansion. The broader context reveals that the US EV market remains dominated by Tesla and traditional automakers, with Korean manufacturers struggling to compete without government incentives.  

The **legal rulings on AI** further complicate the innovation landscape. Two recent court cases have established that AI lacks human intelligence and cannot be considered an author or hold confidential attorney-client communications. These rulings reinforce the idea that AI is fundamentally a tool created by humans, not an independent entity. This legal clarity may slow the pace of AI-driven innovation but also mitigates risks associated with overpromising AI capabilities.  

The **UK’s proposed executive powers to regulate tech** without parliamentary scrutiny represent a dangerous trade-off between rapid response and democratic accountability. While aimed at addressing harms like AI-generated content, these measures raise concerns about potential overreach and erosion of democratic processes. Civil liberties groups argue that bypassing parliamentary debate undermines the legislative process, potentially leading to disproportionate regulations.  

Finally, the **Middle East conflict** is disrupting global shipping routes, particularly through the Strait of Hormuz, where 20% of the world’s oil and gas transit. This escalation poses a significant threat to global energy security and economic stability, with European nations indirectly affected through energy imports. The disregard for international law by Iran and the U.S. administration’s stance further exacerbate the crisis, highlighting the fragility of global supply chains.
