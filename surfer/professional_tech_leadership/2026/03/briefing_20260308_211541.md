This reflects a broader trend where **manufacturers may need to reconsider EV production strategies**, focusing on domestic manufacturing to avoid tariffs. 2. **AI-Augmented Scrum Teams**: The integration of autonomous AI agents into Scrum teams requires redefined roles, with Product Owners remaining exclusively human while Developers transition to "Agent Orchestrators." This shift necessitates **investing in human team members** to supervise and validate AI contributions effectively. Delivery leaders must balance **empirical process control** with human oversight to avoid burnout and ensure ethical AI deployment.  

3. **Corporate Jargon and Decision-Making**: A Cornell study found a strong correlation between susceptibility to corporate jargon (e.g., "synergizing paradigms") and poor decision-making. Leaders must prioritize **critical thinking** over buzzwords, as jargon can hinder analytical thinking and job performance. This trend affects career development, where Product Owners and Delivery Managers must avoid over-reliance on jargon to build trust and alignment.  

4. **Geopolitical Risks to Critical Infrastructure**: Military GPS jamming disrupts commercial shipping and aviation, with incidents increasing dramatically. This poses a **systemic risk to global trade**, particularly for shipping lanes like the Strait of Hormuz. Leaders in delivery and consulting must diversify navigation technologies to reduce dependency on GPS, investing in resilient alternatives.  

5. **AI-Driven Workforce Reductions**: Block’s layoffs of nearly half its workforce are justified by AI efficiency, but critics argue this is "AI-washing." The actual impact of AI on job displacement is limited, with Goldman Sachs estimating only 5,000-10,000 jobs lost monthly across all U.S. sectors. Leaders must focus on **upskilling and reskilling** rather than framing cuts as AI-driven, to maintain team morale and ethical standards.
