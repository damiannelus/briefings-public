Bielik's development, while promising, faces significant hurdles: a lack of specialized knowledge, insufficient computational power (GPU), and data scarcity. These challenges are not unique to Poland but highlight a global race to secure technological independence. The strategic investment in Bielik is thus not just about creating another AI model; it's about building a foundation for future innovation, reducing reliance on foreign platforms, and enhancing national security in the digital age.

This would require substantial, sustained investment in R&D and talent acquisition.

This could lead to increased reliance on foreign AI solutions, potentially compromising sensitive applications and limiting economic opportunities for Polish companies.

Prioritizing quick wins over foundational research and talent pipelines could cripple Poland's ability to compete strategically in the future, making it dependent on foreign technology for decades.
