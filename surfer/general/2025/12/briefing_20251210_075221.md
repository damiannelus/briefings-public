## Macro Spotlight – Deep Dive  
**AI‑Enabled Innovation as the New Competitive Edge**  
The Google Workspace research report shows that organisations fully embracing AI see a **57 %** lift in innovation, a **39 %** drop in routine work, and a **65 %** boost in creative output (source: [Google AI Workplace Report](https://blog.google/products/workspace/gemini-ai-workplace-research-report/)). These metrics are not mere vanity figures; they translate into measurable business outcomes: faster product cycles, higher employee engagement, and a stronger market moat.  

In a world where digital transformation is a survival imperative, the AI adoption curve is the new bellwether for organisational resilience. If the trend accelerates, firms that lag will face a widening performance gap, while those that integrate AI early can command premium pricing and attract top talent. Conversely, a slowdown—perhaps due to regulatory constraints or talent shortages—could stall innovation pipelines and erode competitive advantage. A reversal, driven by privacy backlash or cost overruns, would force a costly re‑engineering of core processes.  

For **Leaders, investors, and managers of innovation**, this means:  
- **Leaders** must champion a culture that treats AI as a strategic enabler, not a cost centre, and allocate at least 10 % of R&D spend to AI pilots.  
- **Investors** should screen portfolios for AI maturity scores, prioritising firms that demonstrate tangible productivity gains (≥ 30 % time savings) and revenue lift (≥ 20 %).  
- **Innovation managers** need to embed cross‑functional AI squads that can iterate on use‑cases, ensuring that each deployment yields a clear KPI (e.g., cycle‑time reduction, defect‑rate drop).  

The 2025 banking sector’s shift from acquiring new accounts to deepening existing relationships (see “Kont przybywa szybciej niż klientów”) mirrors this AI narrative: depth over breadth. If banks can harness AI to personalise offerings, they will likely see a similar **+57 %** innovation boost, reinforcing the case for AI as a catalyst for sustainable growth.
