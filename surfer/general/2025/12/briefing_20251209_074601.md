## Macro Spotlight – Deep Dive  
**AI Investment Bubble vs. Banking Productivity**  
The Wall Street euphoria around generative AI has culminated in **$1.7 trillion** of capital flowing into the sector over the next two years (źródło: ["Bańka na Wall Street czy supercykl? \"Technologia wyprzedziła zdolność ludzi\""](https://www.bankier.pl/wiadomosc/Banka-AI-na-Wall-Street-czy-supercykl-Technologia-wyprzedzila-zdolnosc-ludzi-9051812.html?utm_source=RSS&utm_medium=RSS&utm_campaign=Wiadomosci)). Yet, Databricks reports that only **5 %** of AI initiatives achieve P&L impact, while a well‑governed data foundation can unlock a **100‑150 bps** lift in ROE (źródło: ["Powering Growth: How Data and AI Are Rewiring Productivity in Banking and Payments"](https://www.databricks.com/blog/powering-growth-how-data-and-ai-are-rewiring-productivity-banking-and-payments)).  

If the bubble bursts, banks may slash AI spend, causing ROI to stagnate and forcing a reevaluation of cost‑of‑capital models. For leaders, this means tightening governance, prioritizing high‑impact use cases, and monitoring the **5 %** success rate as a KPI.  

Should the bubble sustain, disciplined adoption of Databricks’ AgentBricks could deliver the projected **150 bps** ROE lift, but banks will need to absorb higher operating costs and potentially higher interest rates (e.g., the **7 %** benchmark from Credit Agricole). Leaders must balance the upside of productivity gains against the risk of elevated financing costs.  

In a scenario where the bubble stalls, moderate AI investment growth will require banks to focus on incremental efficiencies, aligning talent acquisition (the **36 %** hiring trend) with cost‑control measures. For managers, this translates to a dual mandate: accelerate hiring in high‑growth tech roles while tightening spend on low‑impact AI pilots.  

Ultimately, the interplay between AI capital, governance, and talent dynamics will dictate whether banks can convert the **$1.7 trillion** influx into sustainable profitability.
