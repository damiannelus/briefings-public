A robust compliance framework, anchored in the U.S. data‑protection statutes, will be essential to mitigate cross‑border risk.

If the U.S. and China finalize the hand‑over, U.S. firms will gain a foothold in a massive user base, but must invest in secure data pipelines. The cost of compliance could reach **$200 m** in the first year, but the upside is a **$5 bn** annual revenue stream from advertising.  

**Scenario 2: Deal Stalls or Reverses**  
A stalled agreement would trigger a regulatory backlash, potentially costing U.S. firms **$1 bn** in lost market share. The uncertainty would also dampen investor confidence, pushing valuations of U.S. tech firms down by **3–5 %**.  

**Scenario 3: Partial Roll‑back**  
A partial rollback—retaining data in the U.S. but allowing Chinese ownership—would create a hybrid model. This could reduce compliance costs by **$50 m** but would still expose firms to geopolitical risk, potentially leading to a **2 %** drop in user growth.  

**Implications**  
- **For business leaders**: Build a data‑governance playbook that can pivot between U.S. and Chinese regulatory regimes.  
- **For investors**: Allocate a **5 %** risk‑adjusted capital buffer to cover potential compliance overruns.  
- **For innovation managers**: Prioritize modular data architecture to enable rapid compliance updates.
